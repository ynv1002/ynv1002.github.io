Introduction¶

New Orleans is a vibrant tourist destination known for its music, food, and festivals, attracting millions of visitors each year. Consequently, short-term rentals (STRs) have flourished, with both local and corporate investors capitalizing on the growing demand for temporary lodging. However, concerns over housing affordability, corporate buyouts, and neighborhood disruption have led to increasing regulation of STRs by the city.

In 2023, a new ordinance was introduced limiting STR permits to one per residential block and requiring property managers to reside on-site (https://www.wwno.org/local-regional-news/2025-02-25/explaining-new-orleans-short-term-rental-saga). At the same time, New Orleans faces crime challenges, and some community members argue that STRs contribute to increased crime rates due to the transient nature of tenants.

Our project aims to explore the relationship between short-term rentals and crime rates, investigating whether high rental densities correlate with increased crime reports and whether crime levels affect STR application approvals.

Research Questions:¶

  1. What variables are most strongly correlated with a accepted application?
  2. Do high-density STR areas experience more crime?
  3. Are STR applications more likely to be denied in high-crime areas, especially where disturbances are reported?

5. Data Challenges & Next Steps¶

Challenges Faced:¶

  1. Missing Data: Many applications are missing bedroom and guest occupancy limits, which could affect rental density analysis.
  2. Geolocation Issues: Some records had invalid coordinates (-1.00000, -1.00000), making it harder to map STR locations accurately.
  3. Date Formatting Issues: Application and issue dates were originally stored as text, requiring conversion.
  4. Bias in Data: Approval rates may be influenced by new regulations in 2023, meaning trends before/after should be compared.

Next Steps:¶

  1. Merge crime data with STR applications to analyze crime trends in high-density rental areas.
  2. Filter STR approvals before & after the 2023 ordinance to see its impact.
  3. Analyze STR locations vs. crime incidents on a heatmap for visual correlation.
  4. Investigate why certain applications are denied—whether it's zoning laws, high crime, or other factors.
